### **Phase 1: Production (Waffles & Cones)**

#### **1\. The Dough Requirement**

An industrial mixer contains a specific volume of waffle dough. We need to calculate how much dough is actually used to bake a single cone.

* **The Cone Dimensions:** The baked cone has a **base radius (**$r$**) of 21 mm** and a **vertical height (**$h$**) of 28 mm**.  
* **The Thickness:** The walls of the cone are **1 mm thick**.  
* **Approximation:** Assume the volume of dough \= (Curved Surface Area of the cone) $\\times$ (Thickness).

Question: Calculate the volume of dough (in $mm^3$) required to produce one such cone.  
(Hint: First find the slant height $l$).

#### **2\. The Waffle Basket (Frustum)**

The factory introduces a "Waffle Basket" for sundaes.

* **Shape:** It has a flat circular base and a curved side that flares outwards (a frustum).  
* **Dimensions:**  
  * Bottom (Base) Radius ($r\_1$) \= **21 mm**  
  * Top (Open) Radius ($r\_2$) \= **42 mm**  
  * Vertical Height of the basket ($h$) \= **28 mm**  
* **Note:** The slant height ($l$) here is calculated using the difference in radii ($r\_2 \- r\_1$).

**Question:** Find the Total Surface Area of the basket (Area of the circular base \+ Curved Surface Area of the frustum).
